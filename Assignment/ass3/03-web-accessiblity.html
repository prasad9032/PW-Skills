Web accessiblity

Web accessibility, or eAccessibility is the inclusive practice of ensuring there are no barriers that prevent interaction with, or access to, websites on the World Wide Web by people with physical disabilities,
situational disabilities, and socio-economic restrictions on bandwidth and speed. When sites are correctly designed, developed and edited, more users have equal access to information and functionality.

  1. Screen readers: Software used by blind or visually impaired people to read the content of the computer screen.
  2. Screen magnification software: Allow users to control the size of text and or graphics on the screen.
  3. Text readers: Software used by people with learning disabilities, eye fatigue, or other conditions that affect their ability to read text
  4. Website accessibility software: Can be leveraged by those with disabilities to help them be active online
    . Examples include screen readers and screen magnifiers, which allow those with blindness and low vision to engage with online content.
  5. Technical requirements: Ensure that websites work well with assistive technologies. This includes screen readers that read aloud content, and screen magnifiers that enlarge content
